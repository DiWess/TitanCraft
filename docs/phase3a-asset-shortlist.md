## Coherent combination recommendation

The best currently researched direction is a paid Synty-led combination for environment/architecture/props/wreckage, with Kenney retained only as supplemental CC0 background or fallback material. Synty packs are likely more coherent with each other in polygon density, proportions, texture detail, roughness, edge treatment, and color adaptability than mixing multiple unrelated free packs. However, exact Synty license/export details and exact contents must be verified before purchase.

Kenney-only remains legally clean and Godot-friendly, but the rendered Phase 3A recovery already showed that the current Kenney subset cannot carry the hero ship, terrain, and interactables to the target quality by itself.

## Budget strategies

### Free-only

- Environment: Kenney Nature Kit + optional CC-BY Sketchfab rocks only if attribution is accepted.
- Architecture/wreckage/props: Kenney Space Kit, Modular Space Kit, Space Station Kit.
- Enemy/arm: keep local placeholder or research exact Quaternius/Kenney character pieces.
- Honest quality expectation: unlikely to reach the required Phase 3A visual target without custom modeling or strong human layout work.

### Lean paid ($50–$150)

- Primary spend: SyntyPass one-month access if it legally provides usable asset files for selected packs, or equivalent direct Synty/Unity purchases after license/export review.
- Prioritize: POLYGON Sci-Fi Space + POLYGON Sci-Fi Worlds/City, plus LMHPOLY rocks only if a terrain gap remains.
- Expected benefit: stronger cohesive industrial/wreckage vocabulary and fewer random mixed-pack seams.

### Strong vertical slice ($150–$500)

- Build a coherent Synty-led vertical-slice library: Sci-Fi Space for ship/wreck, Sci-Fi Worlds/City for structures and props, plus a verified volcanic/rocks pack and a verified enemy/mechanical-arm source.
- Use Kenney only as background filler or blockout support.
- Expected benefit: highest chance to satisfy human screenshot review while staying stylized and performant.
