# Approved vs Rejected Patterns

These are text-only pattern records. They define taste targets and known failures before final approved production assets exist.

## Approved Target Patterns

### A-001 — Heavy Salvage Hull

- Classification: reference-only target pattern.
- Target: heavy, crashed, off-white industrial hull with dark graphite interior, broken rectangular panels, exposed ribs, and repair scars.
- Must read as: manufactured survival technology damaged by impact.
- Evidence required for future asset: neutral-grey silhouette PNG, material PNG, source provenance, manifest entry.

### A-002 — Irregular Impact Basin

- Classification: reference-only target pattern.
- Target: volcanic impact basin with raised fractured rim, broken basalt side faces, ash transitions, and depth layers.
- Must read as: natural terrain shaped by crash force, not a level editor platform.
- Evidence required for future asset: front, three-quarter, top, and route-read screenshots.

### A-003 — Practical Modular Workbench

- Classification: reference-only target pattern.
- Target: rectangular industrial workbench built from salvaged modules, clamps, cables, graphite trays, muted orange interact accents, and visible repairs.
- Must read as: usable crafting station in a crash site.

### A-004 — Hostile Biomechanical Scout

- Classification: reference-only target pattern.
- Target: asymmetric dark Galaxabrain Scout with organic-mechanical joints, localized cyan/turquoise sensor glow, and one dominant threat silhouette.
- Must read as: hostile alien tech, not generic robot or cute creature.

### A-005 — Selective State Glow

- Classification: reference-only target pattern.
- Target: glow appears only where state matters: beacon lens, alien weak point, save indicator, or interact cue.
- Must read as: signal and function, not decoration.

## Rejected TitanCraft Failure Patterns

### R-001 — Route Slab Failure

- Problem: routes represented by flat slabs, cards, or ribbons read as artificial board pieces.
- Lesson: guide movement through terrain massing, ash value shifts, silhouettes, props, and negative space instead.
- Future rejection trigger: any route strip that is visually separate from terrain without diegetic purpose.

### R-002 — Toy Hull Failure

- Problem: a ship or hull reads like a small toy because proportions are smooth, simple, or rounded without industrial mass.
- Lesson: fix silhouette and scale first; decals and wear cannot rescue toy proportions.
- Future rejection trigger: capsule hull, oversized cute forms, paper-thin paneling, or decorative glow replacing structure.

### R-003 — Platform Terrain Failure

- Problem: terrain reads as a board, arena platform, or rectangular island rather than volcanic ground.
- Lesson: use irregular basalt masses, broken side faces, raised rims, fractured surfaces, ash blends, and layered depth.
- Future rejection trigger: flat island, pure black floor, rectangular platform, or terrain with no natural breakup.

### R-004 — Pyramid / Panel Ridge Failure

- Problem: repeated pyramid ridges or panel-like terrain ridges look manufactured or procedural in the wrong way.
- Lesson: ridges need erosion, irregular spacing, side-face breakage, and scale variation.
- Future rejection trigger: repeated triangular teeth, panel seams, or ridge grids used as terrain language.

### R-005 — Debug-Color Contamination Failure

- Problem: bright debug colors survive into art review and contaminate material judgment.
- Lesson: debug colors are allowed only in diagnostic captures and must be removed before art-read claims.
- Future rejection trigger: primary-color test materials, unassigned magenta, neon placeholders, or color coding presented as art.

### R-006 — Tests Pass, Art Bad Failure

- Problem: technical validation passes while composition, silhouette, material, or route readability fails.
- Lesson: runtime checks and visual approval are separate gates; reviewers must inspect images directly.
- Future rejection trigger: claiming visual readiness from build/import success alone.

### R-007 — Autonomous Mesh Generation Overconfidence

- Problem: generated meshes are treated as production-ready because an agent produced them without human taste constraints.
- Lesson: generated geometry must pass the same brief, silhouette, material, provenance, and review gates as authored assets.
- Future rejection trigger: unbriefed generated meshes, fake placeholders, or source files with no art-direction evidence.
