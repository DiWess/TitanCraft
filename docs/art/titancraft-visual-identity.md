# TitanCraft Visual Identity

## Canonical Project Director Entrypoint

This document is the canonical Project Director entrypoint for TitanCraft visual identity. It summarizes the approved visual direction for routing, review, and rejection decisions without authorizing Stage A art replacement or changes to gameplay scope.

`docs/visual-style.md`, `docs/visual-technical-bible.md`, and `docs/visual-review-checklist.md` remain supporting references for palette, production details, and review mechanics. If a Project Director prompt needs one visual identity starting point, start here first.

## Core Direction: Polygonal Salvage Sci-Fi

TitanCraft uses **Polygonal Salvage Sci-Fi** for the locked Crash Site MVP: readable, low-to-medium poly science fiction built from damaged human hardware, hostile alien biomatter, volcanic terrain, and practical survival repairs.

The style is:

- **not cartoonish**: avoid rubbery proportions, oversized toy shapes, mascot silhouettes, and joke-like visual language;
- **not photorealistic**: avoid high-frequency realism, scanned surfaces, dense film-detail materials, and realism that hides gameplay readability;
- **not block-based**: avoid voxel, Minecraft-like cubes, grid-block construction, or terrain that reads as destructible blocks;
- **not glossy toy sci-fi**: avoid plastic shine, spotless panels, over-saturated neon, or showroom-clean spaceships;
- **low-to-medium poly**: prefer authored broad planes, bevels, chunky readable forms, and controlled detail density;
- **strong silhouettes**: every production candidate must read clearly in neutral grey before materials or glow are considered;
- **simplified PBR**: use rough, readable material groups with stable albedo/roughness/metalness choices rather than dense texture noise;
- **worn and repaired**: human objects should show visible wear, salvage, patched panels, exposed structure, dirt, scorch, or field repair;
- **selective with glow**: emissive accents are rare functional signals, not full-scene decoration.

## Human Tech Shape Language

Human technology should feel practical, modular, and salvageable:

- broad industrial panels, frames, ribs, clamps, crates, rails, vents, and mechanical hinges;
- readable off-white, graphite, steel, bronze, and functional orange accents;
- angular but usable silhouettes that imply engineered load paths and repair access;
- visible damage, replacement parts, field patches, stripped panels, and exposed internal structure;
- orange reserved for interaction, wayfinding, hazard, or functional markings.

Human technology must not read as luxury spacecraft, sleek fighter craft, real-world branded hardware, or a polished toy kit.

## Alien / Galaxabrain Shape Language

Alien and Galaxabrain visuals should contrast against human salvage:

- asymmetrical biomorphic and biomechanical forms;
- faceted organic masses mixed with metallic or neural structures;
- sharp, twisted, ribbed, or carapace-like silhouettes;
- dark alien bases with restrained violet, cyan, or red energy/biomass accents;
- glow limited to cores, weak points, active energy lines, or beacon-state feedback.

The MVP contains one Galaxabrain Scout enemy type. Visual identity work must not imply multiple production enemy types, new alien factions, or expanded combat scope.

## Volcanic Terrain Rules

Crash Site terrain should support survival readability and visual contrast:

- dark volcanic rock, basalt-like silhouettes, ash, fractured ground, and readable slopes;
- simplified polygonal rock masses rather than noisy photoreal terrain;
- limited alien vegetation or crystals used as guidance and mood, not clutter;
- clear gameplay paths, landmarks, and objective readability over scenic complexity;
- no block terrain, full procedural-world implication, or destructible voxel language.

## Automatic Visual Rejection Patterns

Reject visual candidates or scene proposals that show any of these patterns:

- cartoon mascot proportions or toy-like materials;
- photoreal scan style that conflicts with low-to-medium poly readability;
- block-based, voxel, Minecraft-like, or destructible-grid art direction;
- glossy plastic sci-fi, clean showroom ships, or excessive neon glow;
- silhouettes that only read after materials, outlines, or labels are applied;
- random cubes glued together instead of authored industrial structure;
- paper-thin panels where heavy crash wreckage needs mass and thickness;
- excessive cyan/violet glow that overwhelms functional signals;
- visual claims of Stage A approval without PNG review artifacts and human or visual-reviewer verdict;
- production scene replacement before standalone artifact review, manifest evidence, hashes, and approval are complete.

## Production Gate Reminder

This document defines visual identity only. It does not approve Stage A generated art, does not authorize production scene replacement, and does not change the README-defined Crash Site MVP scope.
